Question 1: Market Analysis

What is happening in the market or environment and why?

Purpose: Gain an unfiltered understanding of external forces before making internal decisions

Key Steps:

  1. List all known and assumed trends
  2. Separate facts from beliefs
  3. Test with fresh data
  4. Map scenarios (optimistic/neutral/pessimistic)

What to Challenge:

  • What might we be missing because it's "obvious"?
  • Where are we using outdated information?

Question 2: Mission Clarity

What have we been asked to achieve and why?

Purpose: Reframe the brief. Is the stated objective the real issue or just a symptom?

Key Steps:

  1. Restate the objective in your own words
  2. Ask: What happens if we do nothing?
  3. Identify who benefits and who resists
  4. Create alternative problem statements

Example:

Asked: "Improve customer service response times"
Real Issue: Product complexity causing unnecessary calls

Question 3: Success Metrics

What outcomes will define success?

Purpose: Separate activities from impact. Define real-world success measures

Key Principles:

  • Define success as if reporting in 2 years
  • Create metrics that prove outcomes
  • Identify unintended consequences
  • Establish balancing metrics

Common Traps:

  • Metrics that are easy to game
  • Winning on paper, losing in reality
  • Single metrics driving wrong behaviors

Question 4: Options Generation

What options do we really have?

Purpose: Escape default thinking. Generate contrasting courses of action

(What everyone assumes)

The Opposite
(Reverse approach)

Option C

(Fastest, cheapest)

Option D

The Moonshot
(Unlimited resources)

Option E

Do Nothing
(Strategic waiting)

Question 5: Stress Testing

How will each option perform under stress?

Purpose: Test paths against adversity. Understand breaking points

Stress Scenarios:

Budget Cut 50%

Key People Leave

Timeline Halved

Competitor Launches

Technology Fails

Market Shifts

Question 6: Early Warning Systems

What could go wrong—and when will we know?

Purpose: Build early detection. Avoid surprise failures

Implementation Steps:

  1. Imagine the project has failed
  2. List five probable causes
  3. Define warning signs 90 days early
  4. Establish trigger actions
  5. Create kill criteria

Example Warnings:

  • Week 4: Less than 10% target signups
  • Sprint 3: Velocity drops 30%
  • Month 2: Overtime exceeds 20%

Question 7: Control & Flexibility

How do we stay in control while remaining flexible?

Purpose: Balance governance with adaptability

Key Elements:

  • Clear decision rights matrix
  • Define what can flex vs. what's fixed
  • Establish feedback loops
  • Enable rapid response to change

Critical Questions:

  • How fast does bad news travel?
  • Are there incentives to hide problems?
  • Who can make quick decisions?

Common Pitfalls to Avoid

Pitfall Description How to Avoid
Confirmation Bias Only seeking supporting evidence Ask "What would disprove this?"
Group Think Avoiding conflict for harmony Assign devil's advocates
Anchoring Stuck on first solution Generate 3 options before choosing
Planning Fallacy Underestimating resources Add 50% buffer to estimates
Sunk Cost Fallacy Continuing due to past investment Review as if starting fresh
